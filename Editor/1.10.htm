1.10 Trains
 

To make your map more attractive you may include railway lines with moving or stationary trains.
 Important information about constructing railroads:
- place rails on flat terrain only.
TIP: Construct your railroad before modeling a new map.

On the generated map you may flatten terrain by using the 'Const' option under the 'Altitudes' tab.

Trains in World War II may behave as follow:
1. N (Nothing) - train remains stationary.
2. B (To the end of railroad and back) - train runs between the beginning and the end of the railroad.
3. EoWL (End of the World, looped) - train runs beyond the map, and appears on the opposite side of     the map.
Remember!
Railroads can only run North <-> South or East <-> West.

Constructing a railroad:

Click on 'Objects' , then Railroad Group and Railroad. Then make a selection from the track options.
Place the railway lines on the map.

If you want your train to run to the end of map (L behavior), rails must be placed from left margin <-> right margin or top margin <-> bottom margin.

When the railroad is complete, a train can be put on it. Click on Objects, 'Railroad Group and then 'Train. From the list on the right, select your locomotive and the set its movement properties. Click on the 'N', 'B' or 'EoWL' button.
The train can Now be put on the rails

Remember!
If you choose movement properties B , your train must have two locomotives.
